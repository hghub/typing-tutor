## The Short Answer

For many households, a PKR 20,000 monthly bill often points toward a system in the rough range of **4kW to 5kW**. But that is only a starting point, not a guaranteed answer.

The real size depends on your actual units consumed, city sun hours, daytime usage, AC load, and how much of the generation you will self-consume instead of export.

## Why the Bill Alone Is Not Enough

Your electricity bill includes more than energy usage. It can include taxes, fixed charges, fuel adjustments, and other items solar does not erase directly. That is why two homes with the same bill can still need different system sizes.

## What Usually Pushes the Size Up

- heavy summer AC use
- low-sun city profile compared with hotter southern cities
- more family members home during the day
- electric pumps, ironing, or other heavier daytime loads
- wanting stronger reduction of the remaining bill

## What Can Keep the Size Lower

- moderate daytime demand
- good appliance efficiency
- high-sun city conditions
- willingness to shift more usage into solar hours

## What a 20,000-Bill User Should Watch Out For

This bill range is where many people get pushed toward a larger system than they really need. The better move is often to compare:

- a full net-billing system
- a slightly smaller self-consumption-focused setup

Under the current policy environment, that comparison matters more than it used to.

## Should You Add Battery at This Bill Level?

Battery is usually not driven by the bill alone. It is driven more by:

- load shedding severity
- need for backup
- how much daytime surplus you want to use later at night
