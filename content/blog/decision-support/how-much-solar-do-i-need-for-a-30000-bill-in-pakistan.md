## The Practical Starting Range

If your monthly electricity bill is around PKR 30,000, many homes land in the rough range of **5kW to 7kW**. But the right answer depends on how the bill is created, not just the headline number.

## When 5kW May Still Be Enough

5kW can still be enough when:

- your daytime usage is disciplined
- summer peaks are seasonal rather than constant
- your home is in a stronger-sun city
- you are willing to keep some post-solar bill instead of chasing zero

## When You May Need 6kW or More

The case for 6kW or more strengthens when:

- multiple ACs run heavily in summer
- family occupancy is high during solar hours
- you want stronger offset of a persistently high unit load
- your winter bill also remains relatively elevated

## The Risk at This Bill Level

This is the zone where users can make two opposite mistakes:

1. **undersizing** and then feeling disappointed by the remaining bill
2. **oversizing** and paying too much for export-heavy generation

That is why scenario comparison matters more than generic “bill to kW” rules.

## Think in Terms of Usage Pattern, Not Ego Size

Some people want the bigger number because it feels safer. But the better outcome is the system that creates the strongest combination of:

- self-consumption
- practical savings
- reasonable payback
- upfront cost discipline

## What About Battery?

At this bill level, battery becomes more relevant if your home faces regular outages or if your larger system would otherwise export a noticeable amount of solar at low value. But it still should be judged separately, not added blindly.
