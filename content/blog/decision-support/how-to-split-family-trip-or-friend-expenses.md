## Where Shared Money Usually Becomes Messy

Trips with friends, family grocery runs, office food orders, shared fuel, or house bills often end with someone doing rough maths in a WhatsApp chat. That usually creates confusion, missed items, or awkward overpayment.

## The Main Problem

People often mix two different situations:

- **Equal split:** everyone should pay the same
- **Unequal split:** one person paid more items or some people should pay different shares

## When a Simple Tool Helps

A good split tool saves time when:

- one person paid the restaurant bill
- multiple friends paid for different parts of a trip
- siblings are sharing a family purchase
- roommates are clearing household costs

## What Makes the Result Easier to Accept

People trust the split more when they can see the line items and not just a random total. Clear numbers reduce awkwardness and remove the feeling that someone guessed the result.
