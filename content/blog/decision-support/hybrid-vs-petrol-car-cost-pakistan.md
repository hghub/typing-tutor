## Why Hybrid Is Often Misjudged

People often reject hybrid because the purchase price is higher. That is understandable, but incomplete. Ownership decisions should compare **total cost over time**, not just the showroom number.

## What Hybrid Can Solve Well

- better fuel economy in heavy city driving
- less dependency on charging infrastructure than EV
- a practical middle ground between old petrol logic and full EV commitment

## What Can Still Weaken the Hybrid Case

- a very high upfront premium relative to the petrol option
- low annual mileage
- mostly highway driving where city-efficiency gains matter less
- unexpected resale or maintenance weakness for the specific model

## The Practical Test

Hybrid usually wins when:

- you drive enough to make fuel savings meaningful
- most trips are urban
- you want lower running cost without EV charging risk

It weakens when you drive less, travel mostly on open roads, or the premium takes too long to recover.

## Use a Total-Cost View

A strong comparison should include:

- purchase price
- fuel price
- annual distance
- city-driving share
- maintenance assumptions
- resale factor
