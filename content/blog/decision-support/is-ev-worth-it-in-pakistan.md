## The Wrong Way to Judge an EV

Many people compare **petrol cost per month** against **electricity cost per month** and conclude that EV wins instantly. That is too simplistic. An EV decision in Pakistan depends on five things at once:

- daily mileage
- home-charging reliability
- load shedding reality
- upfront price premium
- what hybrid alternatives already solve well

## When EV Becomes Stronger

An EV case usually improves when:

- your daily mileage is high enough for fuel savings to compound
- you can charge reliably at home
- your route is mostly predictable
- you do not depend on uncertain public charging access
- you are comparing against a relatively expensive petrol car lifecycle

## When Hybrid Often Beats EV

Hybrid becomes the safer answer when:

- city driving is heavy
- fuel savings matter but charging risk is still uncomfortable
- you want better resale confidence today
- you do not want to redesign your home charging setup

## Why Load Shedding Still Matters

An EV can be cheap to run and still annoying to own if charging routines are unstable. If outages are frequent and backup planning is weak, the convenience and confidence advantage can shift back toward hybrid.

## The Mileage Threshold Problem

If you drive only short distances, the EV price premium may take too long to recover. That is why mileage is critical. A vehicle used for 15 km/day and a vehicle used for 60 km/day should not produce the same recommendation.

## The Better Comparison

Instead of asking "Which one is modern?", ask:

- Which option has the best five-year total ownership cost?
- How reliable is the charging routine?
- How painful would resale uncertainty be for me?
- How much convenience am I willing to trade for lower running cost?
