## The Offer Is Not Only the Salary

A move from one city to another changes much more than your gross pay. It changes living-cost pressure, commute pattern, family rhythm, and how much of the package is actually left for saving or investing.

## What Usually Changes in a Cross-City Move

- rent and household cost baseline
- transport or commute burden
- distance from family support
- quality-of-life friction
- how much the relocation amount really helps

## The Main Mistake

People often compare the new role against the current city lifestyle without adjusting the cost baseline properly. That can make a higher offer feel stronger than it really is.

## What to Ask Before Saying Yes

- How much will the move change monthly cost pressure?
- Will commute and work pattern improve or worsen?
- Is the relocation support enough to matter, or only symbolic?
- Does the long-term growth of the role justify the disruption?
