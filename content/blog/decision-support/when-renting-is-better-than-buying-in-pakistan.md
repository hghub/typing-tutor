## Buying Is Not Automatically the Adult Answer

In many families, buying property is treated as the default sign of financial maturity. But financially, that logic can fail. Renting is often the better choice when flexibility, liquidity, or uncertainty matter more than ownership status.

## Case 1: Your Job or City Is Not Stable Yet

If there is a real chance you will change employers, move cities, or go abroad in the next 2–4 years, renting is usually safer. Buying too early can trap you in a location that no longer matches your work or family needs.

## Case 2: Your Down Payment Would Empty Your Savings

If buying would force you to use nearly all liquid cash, the purchase may be too aggressive. A property can be an asset and still create a dangerous cash position if emergencies, layoffs, health costs, or business slowdowns hit soon after.

## Case 3: Markup Is High and Appreciation Is Uncertain

At high financing costs, the monthly payment may look manageable while the total ownership cost becomes much heavier than expected. If future appreciation does not compensate for that, renting can outperform over the decision horizon.

## Case 4: You Need Optionality More Than Ownership

Renting gives you the option to:

- upgrade quickly when income grows
- move closer to a new office
- change school zones
- reduce housing cost if income becomes volatile

## Case 5: The Property Itself Is a Weak Asset

Some properties look attractive because they are "available now", not because they are high-quality decisions. If the location, resale depth, construction quality, legal clarity, or maintenance burden is weak, renting may still be smarter while you wait for a better purchase candidate.
