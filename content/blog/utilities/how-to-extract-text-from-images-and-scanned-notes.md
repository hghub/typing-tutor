## Retyping Is Usually the Worst Option

If the text already exists inside a screenshot, a photo, or a scanned page, retyping it manually is usually the slowest path. OCR is useful because it turns that visual text back into editable text you can search, clean, translate, or reuse.

## Where OCR Is Especially Useful

- screenshots of messages or notices
- scanned notes and photocopies
- printed forms that need reusable text
- old PDFs without selectable text
- receipts, labels, and photographed handouts

## What to Do After Extraction

Good OCR is not the final step. Once the text is extracted, you often still want to clean spacing, fix line breaks, or copy only the part you actually need. That is why OCR works best when paired with a text-cleaning step.
