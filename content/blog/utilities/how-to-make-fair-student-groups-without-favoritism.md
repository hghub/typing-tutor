## Manual Grouping Often Feels Fairer Than It Actually Is

Teachers usually remember the obvious classroom dynamics, but once skill level, repeated pairings, and group size all interact together, hand-made grouping can drift into imbalance without anyone intending it.

## What a Better Grouping Process Tries to Protect

- mixed ability instead of one strong group and one weak group
- less repeated pairing across rounds
- clear group size rules
- less room for favoritism complaints
