# Study and Career Decision System Brief

Working name:
- `Study & Career Direction Guide Pakistan`

Alternative public names:
- `What Should I Study? Pakistan Guide`
- `Field of Study Decision Guide`
- `Career Path Clarity System`

Status:
- `High-priority build candidate`
- `Safer to launch before the marriage system`

## 1. Purpose

This system should help a Pakistani student or early-career user decide:
- what field of study fits them
- what profession cluster fits them
- what path is realistic under their constraints
- what tradeoffs they are accepting
- what alternatives exist beyond prestige narratives

It should not act like:
- a random quiz
- a â€śyou are meant for Xâ€ť engine
- a degree-ranking machine

## 2. Core Problem It Solves

Most Pakistani students are pulled by:
- parental pressure
- prestige bias
- fear of unemployment
- migration hopes
- confusion between degree and profession
- weak self-knowledge
- weak labour-market understanding

So the system should bring structure to that confusion.

## 3. Design Ethos

The system should be shaped by:
- effort and responsibility
- knowledge-seeking
- consultation
- realism over fantasy
- dignity of useful work
- avoidance of blind imitation

It should be:
- Pakistan-aware
- youth-aware
- class-aware
- gender-aware
- respectful of both university and skill-first routes

## 4. Product Principle

Separate these clearly:
- `field of study`
- `profession`
- `earning path`
- `credential path`

These are often connected, but not identical.

## 5. Decision Model

Use 5 output pillars:

1. `Aptitude Fit`
- analytical
- verbal
- visual/design
- hands-on technical
- people/service
- structured/detail-heavy
- entrepreneurial

2. `Motivation Fit`
- money
- service
- prestige
- stability
- creativity
- independence
- migration
- family tradition

3. `Constraint Fit`
- budget
- city access
- time to earning
- family restrictions
- mobility
- language confidence
- academic stamina

4. `Market Fit`
- employability
- internship dependence
- remote/global potential
- mismatch risk
- licensing barriers
- automation pressure

5. `Path Clarity`
- how clear the user actually is
- whether they are chasing status more than fit
- whether their plan has real steps

## 6. Primary Modes

### A. Student Discovery Mode
For:
- matric/intermediate/A-level stage users

Goal:
- identify strongest study clusters and realistic first paths

### B. University / Degree Choice Mode
For:
- users already leaning toward fields

Goal:
- compare likely return, fit, pressure, and constraints

### C. Career Pivot Mode
For:
- graduates or early workers wanting to change direction

Goal:
- map current strengths to new paths

## 7. Input Architecture

### Step 1: Current Stage
- class / degree status
- age bracket
- city / province
- public/private affordability
- urgency to earn

### Step 2: Natural Tendencies
- what kind of problems do you enjoy?
- what work drains you?
- what comes easier?
- solo vs team preference
- abstract vs practical preference
- tolerance for long study periods

### Step 3: Motivation
- money urgency
- family expectations
- social image
- desire for stability
- desire for creativity
- migration goal
- entrepreneurship desire

### Step 4: Constraints
- family budget
- location constraints
- commute/hostel limits
- gender-specific restrictions
- English confidence
- internet/device access

### Step 5: Work Reality Preference
- office vs field
- government vs private
- local vs remote
- degree-heavy vs skill-heavy
- structured employment vs freelancing

## 8. Output Style

The tool should not output just one answer.

It should output:
- `Primary fit cluster`
- `Safer alternate path`
- `Lower-cost entry path`
- `Earn-sooner path`
- `Longer-study / higher-upside path`

Example output language:
- `Your strongest fit is analytical + self-learning + digital work`
- `You appear to want prestige more than fit; this may create regret`
- `A full four-year degree is not your only good path`
- `Your current constraints make a skill-first route more realistic`

## 9. Recommendation Clusters

Use clusters, not single professions at first.

Examples:
- computing / software / data
- accounting / finance / business operations
- teaching / training / education support
- healthcare support / allied health
- engineering / technical operations
- design / media / communication
- law / policy / admin
- skilled trades / technical certifications
- sales / customer growth / business development

## 10. Pakistan-Specific Logic

The system must explicitly account for:
- cost of degrees
- public vs private opportunity gap
- youth unemployment
- female participation barriers
- mismatch between degree and actual job
- certification and internship importance
- remote-work and freelancing upside
- social pressure toward medicine/engineering/CSS
- underestimation of vocational and applied routes

## 11. Evidence Layer

Design should reflect current realities such as:
- mismatch between field of study and employment
- real employability importance of internships and certifications
- uneven labour-market outcomes by gender, region, and sector

So the tool should weight:
- practical route quality
- actual work exposure
- trainability
- market adaptability

more heavily than just prestige.

## 12. Unsafe Outputs to Prevent

Do not let the tool:
- degrade arts/humanities automatically
- imply only STEM matters
- glorify professions without showing cost and pressure
- shame users for local constraints
- hide low-employability risk
- ignore the option of earning sooner through skills

## 13. Output Modules

The final result should include:
- `Best-fit study clusters`
- `Best-fit work style`
- `What you may be overvaluing`
- `What you may be underestimating`
- `Questions to ask before choosing`
- `What to do in the next 90 days`

## 14. Premium Boundary Later

Possible premium features:
- saved path comparisons
- 2-year and 5-year roadmap
- parent discussion summary
- field-by-field comparison reports
- multi-path scenario planner

## 15. Expert Review Requirements

Before public trust positioning, review with:
- `1 career counselor or education advisor`
- `1 Pakistan labour-market-aware reviewer`
- `2-3 Pakistani students / young professionals`

Validation questions:
- does this reduce confusion?
- does it respect class and gender realities?
- does it avoid prestige bias?
- does it show alternatives honestly?

## 16. Build Order

1. Define cluster taxonomy
2. Write question bank
3. Build rule engine for aptitude + motivation + constraints
4. Add market-fit weights
5. Create output narratives
6. Add path comparison and next-step planning

## 17. Launch Guidance

This can likely be your next major serious decision system after the investment and solar class of tools.

It has:
- strong youth utility
- strong Pakistan relevance
- lower ethical risk than the marriage tool
- high SEO potential through supporting guides

## 18. Non-Negotiable Product Rule

This tool should help users become:
- clearer
- more realistic
- less prestige-driven
- more action-oriented

If it only tells them what sounds impressive, it has failed.
