# Trauma Recovery Decision System Brief

Recommended public name:
- `Steady Again`

Strong alternatives:
- `After the Shock`
- `Recovery Guide`
- `Sukoon Recovery Guide`
- `Come Back Steady`

Recommended positioning:
- `Support after shock, hurt, fear, or difficult life events`

Status:
- `High-sensitivity system`
- `Must not present itself as a therapist`
- `Must have strong crisis escalation logic before launch`

## 1. Purpose

This system should help a Pakistani user who has gone through distressing events such as:
- accident
- robbery
- insult / humiliation
- betrayal
- neglect
- family aggression
- workplace mistreatment
- public shame
- intense fear or shock

The system should help them:
- understand what may be happening to them
- calm and stabilize first
- avoid harmful coping
- follow evidence-based recovery steps
- know when self-help is enough
- know when professional help is needed
- find relevant next sources and local support options

It must not act like:
- a diagnosis engine
- a replacement for therapy
- a religious preacher
- a magic “healing bot”

## 2. Core Product Stance

This should be a:
- `support guide`
- `recovery planner`
- `stabilization and next-step system`

It should not be described as:
- `AI counselor`
- `mental health expert who treats you`

That language creates unsafe expectations.

## 3. Guiding Ethos

The system should quietly draw from:
- evidence-based trauma support
- psychological first aid principles
- emotional regulation science
- trustworthy help-seeking
- Quran-shaped values of patience, dignity, hope, mercy, truthfulness, and seeking remedy
- Shia ethical teachings around sabr, inner steadiness, grief with dignity, and taking means toward healing

Important:
- these influences can shape tone and structure
- but the UI should not pretend to issue religious rulings
- it should feel humane, grounded, calm, and serious

## 4. High-Risk Nature

This system can become dangerous if it:
- fails to detect crisis
- gives false reassurance
- spiritualizes severe symptoms instead of escalating
- encourages isolation
- tells people to “just be patient”
- normalizes abuse
- overuses generic positivity

So this tool must be biased toward:
- safety
- validation
- stabilization
- referral when needed

## 5. Product Thesis

People do not need a machine to tell them:
- “everything will be fine”

They need structured help with:
- what they may be feeling
- what is normal after shock
- what is not normal anymore
- what to do in the next hour, day, week, and month

This should be the central design philosophy.

## 6. Best Product Name Recommendation

My recommendation is:
- `Steady Again`

Why:
- simple
- hopeful but not childish
- not too clinical
- works in Pakistan
- fits both men and women
- can cover accident, robbery, insult, fear, grief, and emotional shock

Suggested subtitle:
- `A calm guide for recovery after shock, fear, hurt, or difficult events`

## 7. Decision Model

The system should use 5 pillars:

1. `Safety`
- am I safe right now?
- is the danger over?
- am I alone?
- do I need urgent help?

2. `Distress Intensity`
- mild
- moderate
- severe
- crisis

3. `Recovery Phase`
- immediate shock
- first few days
- ongoing distress
- stuck / not recovering

4. `Function Impact`
- sleep
- work/study
- concentration
- relationships
- body symptoms
- avoidance / fear

5. `Support Need`
- self-guided recovery is enough for now
- structured support is needed
- professional support is needed
- urgent intervention is needed

## 8. Primary Modes

### A. Immediate Shock Support
For:
- same day / first few days

Goal:
- stabilize
- reduce panic
- restore orientation
- prevent harmful coping

### B. Ongoing Recovery Support
For:
- user is still distressed days or weeks later

Goal:
- identify stuck points
- encourage healthy recovery rules
- reduce shame and confusion

### C. Escalation Mode
For:
- dangerous or severe symptoms

Goal:
- stop the self-help flow
- route to urgent professional or trusted-person help

## 9. Input Architecture

### Step 1: What happened?
Event type:
- accident
- robbery / attack
- insult / humiliation
- family conflict
- workplace event
- betrayal
- neglect
- other

### Step 2: When did it happen?
- today
- last few days
- last few weeks
- long ago but still affecting me

### Step 3: What is happening in you now?
- shaking / panic
- numbness
- racing thoughts
- shame
- fear
- anger
- replaying the event
- poor sleep
- avoiding people / places
- body tension
- hopelessness

### Step 4: Functional impact
- eating
- sleep
- prayer/routine
- work/study
- family functioning
- ability to leave home

### Step 5: Safety and crisis screen
- thoughts of self-harm
- thoughts of harming others
- cannot control panic
- hearing/seeing things not there
- not safe at home
- severe domestic violence or coercion
- medical injury not treated

## 10. Recovery Stage Logic

### Stage 1: Stabilize
First priority:
- safety
- breathing
- grounding
- hydration
- trusted human contact
- reducing overload

### Stage 2: Understand
Explain:
- shock reactions can be normal
- fear, crying, numbness, replaying, poor sleep can happen
- not every intense reaction means permanent damage

### Stage 3: Recover
Guide:
- routines
- sleep protection
- movement
- limited news exposure
- not isolating completely
- gradual return to normal tasks
- values-based steadiness

### Stage 4: Escalate when needed
If symptoms remain strong or disabling:
- encourage professional assessment
- normalize help-seeking
- route to specific resource types

## 11. Output Style

Preferred outputs:
- `You look shaken, but the immediate focus is calming and safety`
- `Your reactions may fit acute stress after a frightening event`
- `You may be stuck in avoidance and replay`
- `This now looks beyond simple self-help`
- `You should not carry this alone`

Never say:
- `You definitely have PTSD`
- `You are fine`
- `Just be patient`
- `This is only weak faith`
- `You do not need help`

## 12. Recovery Guidance Modules

The system should generate a plan under these headings:

### A. What may be happening
Short explanation of likely stress reaction

### B. What to do in the next 10 minutes
- grounding
- breathing
- hydration
- move to safe place
- contact one stable person

### C. What to do today
- reduce overwhelm
- do not repeatedly retell if it makes panic worse
- protect sleep
- avoid substances
- write key facts if needed

### D. What to do this week
- restore small routines
- gentle movement
- avoid total withdrawal
- identify one safe support person
- notice if symptoms are improving or worsening

### E. What signals mean you need more help
- symptoms worsening
- work/study collapse
- ongoing panic
- intrusive memories
- severe sleep breakdown
- hopelessness
- self-harm thoughts

## 13. Scientific Guidance Layer

This system should be based on principles consistent with:
- psychological first aid
- trauma-informed care
- PTSD recognition guidance
- healthy coping and gradual recovery

It should especially encode:
- safety first
- calm first
- connection matters
- practical support matters
- meaning and hope matter
- forcing intense retelling early can be harmful
- severe persistent symptoms need professional care

## 14. Faith-Informed Layer

Without presenting itself explicitly as a religious authority, the tool can quietly reflect:
- patience with dignity, not passivity
- hope without denial
- grief without collapse
- seeking remedy as part of trust in Allah
- avoiding despair
- taking practical means toward healing

It should never imply:
- severe trauma is solved by pious language alone
- professional treatment is against trust in Allah

## 15. Shia-Informed Design Themes

The system’s tone can be informed by:
- sabr as steadiness, not paralysis
- grief acknowledged honestly
- inner dignity
- seeking treatment as legitimate
- hope and accountability together

These values should influence:
- wording
- pacing
- recovery guidance
- avoidance of cold secular tone

## 16. Unsafe Outputs to Prevent

The system must not:
- minimize domestic abuse
- encourage silence when danger exists
- give couples/family mediation advice where violence is present
- recommend only spiritual coping for severe symptoms
- encourage revenge, obsessive replay, or humiliation spirals
- tell users to suppress all emotion

## 17. Crisis Escalation Rules

Immediately stop normal flow and shift to crisis mode if user reports:
- self-harm thoughts
- suicidal thinking
- inability to stay safe
- ongoing violence or abuse
- severe dissociation / inability to orient
- psychotic symptoms
- severe medical consequences after an event

Crisis mode should:
- tell user clearly this is beyond self-guided support
- encourage immediate trusted-person contact
- encourage emergency / medical / mental health help
- reduce all reflective content and move to action

## 18. Resource Layer

The tool should not hardcode random numbers without maintenance.

Instead build a maintained resource layer with:
- emergency help guidance
- how to find local psychologist / psychiatrist / counselor
- hospital mental health units where available
- online evidence-based reading
- trusted local support options by city in later versions

Operational rule:
- this resource layer must be reviewed `monthly`

## 19. Product Modes for Launch

### V1
- rule-based support flow
- event classification
- distress classification
- safety escalation
- recovery plan
- resource guidance

### V2
- saved recovery plan
- daily check-ins
- symptom trend graph
- sleep / panic / routine tracking
- city-based help directory

### V3
- specialized flows:
  - robbery
  - road accident
  - humiliation/social shame
  - family emotional neglect
  - workplace abuse

## 20. Premium Boundary Later

Possible premium features:
- saved recovery journal
- 7-day and 30-day recovery plans
- follow-up check-ins
- printable or shareable support summary
- localized curated help directory

## 21. Expert Review Requirements

Do not publish until reviewed by:
- `1 trauma-informed psychologist or counselor`
- `1 psychiatrist or clinical reviewer for escalation logic`
- `1 faith-aware reviewer who understands Shia ethical framing`
- `2-3 Pakistani users with sensitivity to lived realities`

Review questions:
- does it feel humane?
- does it avoid false therapy?
- does it escalate strongly enough?
- does it handle shame and masculinity issues well?
- does it avoid spiritual bypassing?

## 22. Build Order

1. Define crisis-screen rules
2. Define recovery-stage rules
3. Write response templates
4. Build event-specific pathways
5. Build resource architecture
6. Run expert safety review
7. Only then consider public launch

## 23. Non-Negotiable Product Rule

This system must help a distressed user become:
- safer
- calmer
- less alone
- more oriented
- more likely to seek real help when needed

If it only sounds comforting but does not improve safety and next actions, it has failed.
